Nigeria has underscored its commitment to deepening ties with Indonesia,
highlighting the untapped economic potential and promising avenues for
collaboration.

This was affirmed during the second Indonesia-Africa Forum, that commenced on
September 1 in Bali, Indonesia. Representing President Bola Tinubu, Nigeria’s
Minister of Information and National Orientation, Mohammed Idris, led the
Nigerian delegation to the forum.

The minister reiterated Nigeria’s dedication to fostering a
stronger partnership
with Indonesia, emphasising that both regions would gain significantly from
enhanced cooperation in trade, investment, and the exchange of knowledge and
expertise.

The event, attended by delegates from 22 African countries and five non-African
countries, paid homage to the 1955 Asia-Africa Forum, known as the Bandung
Spirit, which laid the groundwork for Asia-Africa relations.
The meeting called for increased interaction between the continents in terms of
trade, investment, and development cooperation.
